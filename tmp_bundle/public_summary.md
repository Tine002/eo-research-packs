Executive Order 14067, signed March 9, 2022, sets a government-wide approach to digital assets. It outlines policy interests and objectives (Secs. 1–2), assigns interagency coordination to the APNSA and APEP (Sec. 3), instructs research on a potential U.S. central bank digital currency (Sec. 4), and directs assessments related to consumer protection, market integrity, financial stability, illicit finance, and international cooperation (Secs. 5–8). It does not itself authorize new spending or create new agencies. Primary source: Federal Register; backup PDF on GovInfo.
